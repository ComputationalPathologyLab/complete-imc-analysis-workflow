## Overall Conclusion

The current workflow successfully converts representative raw IMC data into segmented cells, processed single-cell marker features, rule-based phenotype labels, spatial neighbor summaries, abundance-normalized spatial enrichment tables, and plasma-cell-focused niche summaries.

The data support an exploratory interpretation of phenotype composition and spatial organization within the representative subset. Plasma-cell-like spatial niche outputs provide the most direct connection to the biological focus of the template study, while enrichment tables help distinguish high raw interaction counts from adjacency patterns that exceed abundance expectation.

A substantial fraction of cells remains `Unknown`, indicating that the final interpretation is best viewed as a technical and exploratory biological summary until phenotype annotation is validated or replaced with a reference-guided classifier.

## Limitations

- Rule-based phenotyping is not equivalent to expert annotation, XGBoost classification, or FlowSOM review.
- Current spatial enrichment uses an abundance-based expectation, not a permutation-based null model.
- The representative subset is small and should not be used for final biological claims.
- Bone-distance and CellCharter-style neighborhood analyses are not yet implemented in this simplified workflow.

## Recommended Next Development

A future extension should add either reference-guided phenotype validation or permutation-based spatial enrichment testing. Bone-mask integration can be added if reproducible bone annotations are available for the target dataset.
